UC Botanical Garden

The UC Botanical Garden is a living museum nestled in the Berkeley Hills. I am the Marketing and Communications Assistant.

  • Print
    Front and back of a rack card for general Garden advertisement. The collection of images on the front is to entice visitors to visit our wonderful collection. I chose to do a collage of images to convey the vast diversity of the Garden, while moving away from tradition rectangular collages. The back contains short, quick information that any new visitor may want to know when hearing about the Garden for the first time.

    Photoshop, InDesign
  • Print
    Two examples of print advertisement for outside publications. The left was printed in the Cal Alumni Association newsletter. The advert highlights Garden rentals due to the newsletter's target audience of post-grad adults and older. The design focuses on the natural beauty of the Redwood Grove with minimal, light text balanced with the light of the photo.
    The right advert was printed in Chinook Book, a local coupon book, so it focuses on opportunities to buy plants at the Garden. The photo was chosen to highlight the diversity of what the Garden offers, as well as an inviting place to visit.

    Photoshop, InDesign, Illustrator
  • Social Media
    The Summer Concert series has accompanying Facebook events to attract outside of normal Garden audiences. This was an accompanying Facebook post enouraging students to purchase a student membership for cheaper concert tickets. The style emulates the earthy and enchanting feel of attenting an outdoors concert surrounded by redwood trees.

    Photoshop, Illustrator
  • Video
    Plea video for a crowdfunding campaign to raise funds to build a discovery cart. The tone of the video is uplifting and inspiring, as is the story and the "ask," to appeal to the exciting things a Discovery Cart may bring to the Garden. Corresponding marketing for this crowdfunding campaign included fliers, signage, social media posts, and emails.

    Premiere, AfterEffects, Illustrator

Xlab

Xlab is a social science lab at UC Berkeley. I have been an Administrative and Technology Assistant since January 2014, focusing on lab branding, recruitment, and marketing.

  • Print
    Four examples of tear-off fliers to be posted around campus for participant recruitment. All give a short, enticing description of what Xlab is and what it can offer to student participants.
    The top two were early attempts to find a consistent style for Xlab where none existed previously. The bottom left was a departure from that style, using color blocks to group content. Leading to the bottom right flier, consistent with the new website and rebranding.

    Illustrator, InDesign
  • Web Design
    Index page for Xlab's website. This website serves as a place for participants and researchers to learn more about what Xlab does and what it can offer them. Thus when designing and organizing content for the website, I had to cater to two audiences with different needs, levels of involvement, and experiences with Xlab. The content is carefully structured so a potential participant who has never heard of Xlab before and a seasoned researcher can navigate to what they need easily.
    I chose the style of the website to reflect the data-driven, human-centered research Xlab facilitates as a social science lab. The index page background image mimics triangulation of data combined with neural synapses and connetedness.

    Dreamweaver, Illustrator
  • Print
    A poster created for the Social Science Matrix Fair. It appeals to graduate students who may want to learn more about rearching at Xlab. The header is to grab the attention of passersby and the body is for those who stop and want to read more.

    Illustrator, InDesign
  • Video
    Recruitment video for Xlab participants. This video outlines what it is to participate at Xlab with simple, 2D animation.

    AfterEffects, Illustrator
