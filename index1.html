    Two examples of print advertisement for outside publications. The left was printed in the Cal Alumni Association newsletter. The advert highlights Garden rentals because the target audience is post-grad adults and older. Xlab

Xlab is a social science lab at UC Berkeley. I have been an Administrative and Technology Assistant since January 2014, focusing on lab branding, recruitment, and marketing.

  • Print
    Four examples of tear-off fliers to be posted around campus for participant recruitment. All give a short, inticing description of what Xlab is and what it can offer to student participants.
    The top two were early attempts to find a consistent style for Xlab where none existed previously. The bottom left was a departure from that style, using color blocks to group content. Leading to the bottom right flier, consistent with the new website and rebranding.

    Illustrator, InDesign
  • Web Design
    Index page for Xlab's website. This website serves as a place for participants and researchers to learn more about what Xlab does and what it can offer them. Thus when designing and organizing content for the website, I had to cater to two audiences with different needs, levels of involvement, and experiences with Xlab. The content is carefully structured so a potential participant who has never heard of Xlab before and a seasoned researcher can navigate to what they need easily.
    I chose the style of the website to reflect the data-driven, human-centered research Xlab facilitates as a social science lab. The index page background image mimics triangulation of data combined with neural synapses and connetedness.

    Dreamweaver, Illustrator
